<p><p>Eddy current brakes utilize electromagnetic induction to create resistance and slow down or stop motion without physical contact. The linear eddy current brakes are designed for applications requiring straight-line motion, typically found in trains and industrial machinery, providing smooth and efficient braking. In contrast, rotary eddy current brakes are used in applications with rotational motion, such as in motors and generators, allowing precise control of speed and torque. Both types enhance safety and reduce wear on mechanical components.</p></p>
