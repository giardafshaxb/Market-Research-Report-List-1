<p><p>The Group B Streptococcus (GBS) infection treatment market is categorized into three primary types: prenatal-onset GBS disease, early-onset GBS disease, and late-onset GBS disease. Prenatal-onset refers to infections that occur in the mother during pregnancy, potentially affecting the fetus. Early-onset GBS disease manifests within the first week of life, usually linked to colonization during labor. Late-onset GBS disease occurs after the first week and up to three months of age, often resulting from postnatal transmission. Each category requires distinct therapeutic approaches.</p></p>
