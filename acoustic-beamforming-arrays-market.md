<p><p>Acoustic beamforming arrays are classified into two-dimensional and three-dimensional markets based on their spatial configurations. Two-dimensional arrays operate on a plane, efficiently directing sound waves to specific locations in horizontal and vertical dimensions, making them ideal for applications like conference systems and outdoor events. In contrast, three-dimensional arrays extend the capability to spatially focus sound in all directions, enhancing audio quality in complex environments like theaters or concert halls, allowing for immersive sound experiences and precise sound localization.</p></p>
